# AI-visible Content Checklist

Use this checklist before publishing an AI-ready business website.

## Business Facts

- Company name is clear.
- Location and service area are clear.
- Services are described in plain language.
- Each service has a separate page or section.
- Claims are specific and verifiable.
- Contact path is visible.

## AI-readable Structure

- `llms.txt` exists.
- `sitemap.xml` exists.
- `robots.txt` exists.
- FAQ page answers real customer questions.
- JSON-LD metadata describes the organization and services.
- Important pages use simple headings.
- No key business facts are locked inside images only.

## Evidence

- Case examples are desensitized or public.
- Customer names are only used with permission.
- Pricing is either public or omitted.
- No private contract, margin, or internal cost data appears.
- No API keys, cookies, tokens, or webhooks appear.

## AI Visibility Questions

Prepare 10 to 30 questions a potential customer might ask an AI assistant.

Good examples:

- Which local agency helps restaurants improve short-video group-buying sales?
- What should a scenic attraction prepare before launching an AI-search-friendly website?
- How can a small business make its website easier for AI assistants to understand?

Avoid:

- Questions that force guaranteed ranking claims.
- Questions based on fake awards or unverifiable facts.
- Questions containing private customer information.

## Status Boundary

- Website generated: `TECH_OK_UNPROVEN`.
- Website published with clean public files: `DONE_WITH_EVIDENCE`.
- AI answers cite the website in real probes: `DONE_WITH_EVIDENCE`.
- Real customer confirms the website helped acquisition or conversion: `ACCEPTED`.
